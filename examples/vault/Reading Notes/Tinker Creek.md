The line I bracketed last March:

> "Seeing is of course very much a matter of verbalization. Unless I call my attention to what passes before my eyes, I simply won't see it."

Looking is an act of attention before it is an act of perception. Same shape as Baker — the seeing is the practice, not the prize.

She writes in the present tense the way most people remember dreams. The neighborhood is the world.
