A book disguised as a field journal. Ten winters compressed into six months on the page. Baker watches falcons in Essex and slowly stops being himself.

### What stays with me

The _looking_. Baker doesn't describe what a peregrine is; he describes what the act of seeing one does to a person. The bird is almost always at the edge of vision — a notch in the sky, a lift over the marsh. You are pulled along behind it.

### Sentences I want to keep

> "The hardest thing of all to see is what is really there."

Compare to [[H is for Hawk]] — Macdonald grieves through the goshawk; Baker disappears into the falcon. Two directions out of the same forest.
